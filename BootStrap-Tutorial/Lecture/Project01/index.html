Samosa is a popular Indian snack featuring a crispy, golden-brown outer layer and a deliciously spiced potato filling

The samosa, as a concept, originated in the Middle East, specifically in the Persian and Central Asian regions, around the 10th century. It was a savory pastry, often filled with meat and spices, and was known as "sambosa". While the exact inventor is unknown, historical records suggest it was a popular snack among travelers and merchants in that area.
